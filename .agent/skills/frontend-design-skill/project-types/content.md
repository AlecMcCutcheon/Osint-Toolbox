# Project Type: Content

Use for docs, portfolios, editorial layouts, galleries, case studies, knowledge bases, and reading-first experiences.

## Priority order

1. Readability and pacing
2. Content hierarchy
3. Navigation and findability
4. Visual rhythm
5. Brand tone

## Research focus

Study:
- typographic systems that support long-form reading
- navigation models for archives, categories, and deep pages
- how strong editorial or portfolio sites create rhythm
- image handling, captions, metadata, and section transitions

## Questions to resolve

- Is the goal to read, browse, discover, compare, or showcase?
- Is the content updated frequently?
- Are visuals or text doing most of the work?
- Should the experience feel archival, editorial, premium, technical, or personal?

## Common failure modes

- oversized hero moments that hurt reading flow
- weak table of contents or navigation for long content
- gallery styling with no narrative structure
- decorative typography that reduces legibility

## Good outcomes

A strong content experience supports comprehension, exploration, and memory through rhythm, hierarchy, and well-paced visual emphasis.
