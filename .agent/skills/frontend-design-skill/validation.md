# Validation

Before finalizing, review the design against this checklist.

## Context validity

- Is the direction clearly tied to the stated user, task, and product?
- Are assumptions labeled instead of hidden?
- Would the recommendation still make sense if questioned by a product designer or founder?

## UX validity

- Is the main action obvious?
- Is information hierarchy clear within 5 seconds?
- Are the top 1-3 user tasks clearly supported?
- Is there unnecessary cognitive load, decorative clutter, or ambiguity?
- Are empty, loading, error, success, and disabled states covered?
- Does the layout use realistic content rather than generic placeholders?

## Research validity

- Was category research actually reflected in the solution?
- Are there clear competitor weaknesses being avoided?
- Is the design distinctive in a way that still respects domain expectations?
- Does the design borrow principles, not just styling, from references?

## Visual validity

- Is the typography intentional and appropriate?
- Is spacing consistent and well-tiered?
- Is the palette restrained and purposeful?
- Does motion support comprehension rather than distract?
- Does the design avoid generic AI tropes and overused startup patterns?

## Accessibility and responsiveness

- Is contrast sufficient?
- Are focus states clear?
- Are touch targets usable?
- Does the design work at small widths without simply shrinking everything?
- Are keyboard and reduced-motion expectations considered?

## Implementation validity

- Is the UI feasible in the requested stack?
- Are performance-heavy effects justified?
- Are content, assets, and components real enough to ship or demo credibly?
- If the user asked for code, is the code working and production-minded?

## Anti-generic audit

Reject and revise if the design relies on any of these without strong justification:
- default three-card feature sections
- centered marketing page clichés everywhere
- decorative gradients with weak content structure
- visual novelty without clearer task flow
- random asymmetry with no compositional purpose
- type or motion choices that fight readability
- generic copy that could describe any startup

## Final review question

Ask yourself:

Would this design still feel thoughtful, specific, and defensible if the colors were temporarily removed?

If not, the structure and reasoning probably need another pass.
